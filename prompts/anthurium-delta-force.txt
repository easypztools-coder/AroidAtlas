SPECIES MORPHOLOGY

--------------------------------

BOTANICAL ACCURACY REQUIREMENTS

Critical identifying traits for Anthurium 'Delta Force':

- Triangular (deltoid) leaf shape with a sharply acuminate apex — the single most defining trait; no other common Anthurium shares this geometry
- Bold, luminous white venation against a deep dark-green blade — high contrast inherited from A. clarinervium
- Leathery, coriaceous leaf surface; slightly velvety adaxially, with a subtle sheen
- Flat arcuate-sagittate leaf base; undulating leaf margins
- No lobing whatsoever — inherits the unlobed blade of A. clarinervium, not the pedatisect lobes of A. pedatoradiatum
- Compact, self-heading, upright clumping growth form — not climbing, not vining

Morphology:

Leaf Shape:
Strongly triangular (deltoid) with a sharply pointed acuminate apex and a wide arcuate-sagittate base; undulating margins; the triangular geometry is immediately recognisable and does not fully develop until the fourth or fifth leaf

Leaf Ratio:
Moderately elongated deltoid, 20–30 cm long; broad at the base narrowing sharply and evenly to the tip; adult leaves broad enough to display prominent venation clearly

Texture:
Leathery and coriaceous; slightly velvety on the adaxial surface; firm and substantial — not thin, papery, or floppy

Petiole:
Terete (round cross-section); mid-green to pale green; proportionally long relative to the leaf blade; smooth surface

Growth Habit:
Self-heading, upright, compact clumping habit; terrestrial velvet anthurium type; does not climb or vine; produces new leaves from a central crown

Mature Size:
40–60 cm overall height and spread; leaves 20–30 cm long; a tidy, architectural houseplant

Origin:
Deliberately hybridised by Steve Nock of Ree Gardens, Miami, Florida, USA, in the 1990s; a select seedling from the cross Anthurium clarinervium × Anthurium pedatoradiatum; Delta Force was the only seedling from the cross to produce the distinctive deltoid leaf with the characteristically narrow tip

Habitat:
Cultivated tropical houseplant; thrives in bright indirect light with high humidity; well-draining aroid mix; does not tolerate waterlogging

The specimen of Anthurium 'Delta Force' should immediately be recognizable to an experienced collector.

NEGATIVE CONSTRAINTS

Do not generate:

- Climbing, vining, or hemiepiphytic growth form
- Yellow, cream, or green venation (veins must be striking white)
- Variegation of any kind on the leaf blade
- Leaves without the triangular deltoid geometry
