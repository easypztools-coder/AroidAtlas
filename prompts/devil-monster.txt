BOTANICAL ACCURACY REQUIREMENTS

Critical identifying traits for Monstera "Devil Monster":

- Mint-coloured variegation inherited from the Kunzo White Monster lineage
- Rigid, deeply lobed leaf shape inherited from Burle Marx's Flame
- Structural, architectural leaf appearance inherited from Jungle Min
- Natural mutation originally discovered in China, referred to as "Circuit Board Monstera"
- Stabilized variegation pattern through years of selective propagation
- Highly sought-after cultivated variety that gained popularity in 2024

Morphology:

Leaf Shape:
Rigid, deeply lobed with narrow flame-shaped segments; structural and architectural appearance combining traits from Burle Marx's Flame and Jungle Min

Leaf Ratio:
Moderately elongated with deep sinuses between lobes; typical Monstera proportions with more rigid structure

Texture:
Firm and rigid; thick leaf substance supporting the deeply lobed structure

Petiole:
Standard Monstera-type petiole; proportionally matched to leaf size

Growth Habit:
Climbing hemiepiphyte typical of the Monstera genus; benefits from support structure

Mature Size:
Comparable to standard Monstera deliciosa but with more compact, rigid growth habit

Origin:
China (originally discovered as a natural mutation, initially called "Circuit Board Monstera"); propagated and stabilized by a private collector over several years; rebranded in Thailand as "Devil Monster" in 2024

Habitat:
Cultivated tropical houseplant; thrives in bright indirect light with high humidity; well-draining aroid mix
